3. HOW WE USE COOKIES AND SIMILAR TECHNOLOGIES

We and our third party partners use Cookies, pixel tags, and similar technologies to collect information about your browsing activities and to distinguish you from other users of our Services in order to enhance your experience and measure and improve our advertising effectiveness. Cookies are small files of letters and numbers that we store on your browser or device, containing information that is transferred to your device.

  • Strictly necessary Cookies: These Cookies are essential to make our Services available to you; for example, to provide login functionality, user authentication, and security. Without these Cookies, the Services cannot be provided.
  • Functional Cookies: These Cookies recognize you when you return to our Website, allowing us to personalize our content for you and remember your preferences (for example, your choice of language).
  • Analytical, performance, or advertising Cookies: We use these Cookies for analytics purposes to operate, maintain, and improve our Services as well as to measure and improve our advertising effectiveness. Third party analytics providers, such as Google Analytics and Amplitude, help us understand user interactions. For instance, Google Analytics uses first-party Cookies to track user interactions which are then compiled into reports that help improve our Service and Website. You can opt out of Google Analytics by visiting this link or via Google’s Ads settings.

You can block Cookies by setting your browser to block some or all Cookies. However, blocking all Cookies (including strictly necessary ones) may prevent you from using our Services.
